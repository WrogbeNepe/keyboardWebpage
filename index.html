Raise of Mechanical Keyboards

Mechanical Keyboards are popular because they're a lot more durable and comfortable than an electrical keyboard. They have also cought the eyes of gamers and streamers who view these keyboards as something that can give them an edge on compatition.

Attributes

Mechanical keyboard keys can bounce back a lot more effectively. They are not just reliant on electrical signals they can withstand a lot more key clicks because the only thing that can be seriously damaged is the moving parts, which can be easily repaired unlike an electrical keyboard.

Lifespan

One of the major benefits of a mechanical keyboard is they have a longer lifespan than other types of keyboards. These kinds of keyboards are tested to last 30 and 70 million keypresses. Comparatively, membrane keyboards will reach the end of their operational lifespan after around 5 million presses.

Keyboard Types

40% Keyboard

A keyboard with only 49 keys as opposed to the 101 keys in regular keyboards.

60% Keyboard

The most compact mechanical keyboards are known as 60% keyboards. They pack a lot of power and comfort into their small size.

75% Keyboard

This keyboard contains 70-75% of the width of a full-size keyboard, but retain the F key row at the top.

100% Keyboard

Full-size mechanical keyboards (with 10-Key number pads).

Cherry Red Switch

CHERRY MX Red and CHERRY MX Red RGB switches are linear-style switches. Their travel path doesn't have the bump used to provide tactile feedback.

Gateron Brown Switch

Gateron Browns are a tactile switch that actuate around 45g and bottom out at around 55g. They are weighted similarly to Cherry MX Browns.

Kailh Switch

Kailh switches are speed switches which are essentially shortened versions of existing MX-style switches, allowing for faster actuation and intended for gaming.

Durock Switch

Durock is a tactile clone of the Cherry MX switch, with a characteristic similar to an Cherry MX Ergo Clear or a softer Zealio V2.
